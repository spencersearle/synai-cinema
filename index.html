§ 01 · a guide for the curious

Finding Answers

Not knowing is the beginning, not the end. The scriptures teach that wisdom is asked for — faithfully, patiently, and often out loud. Spin the compass below, and let it point you somewhere honest.

"If any of you lack wisdom, let him ask of God, that giveth to all men liberally, and upbraideth not; and it shall be given him." — James 1:5
✨

Ask, Then Ask Again

"Ask, and ye shall receive; knock, and it shall be opened unto you." Questions are not weakness — they are the door. (3 Nephi 14:7)

📚

Study It Out

"You must study it out in your mind; then you must ask me if it be right." Revelation rewards the prepared thinker. (D&C 9:8)

🌱

Plant the Seed

Alma taught that faith begins as an experiment — a seed planted, watered, and watched. Truth swells when given room to grow. (Alma 32)

💬

Sincere Heart, Real Intent

Moroni's promise is a blueprint for every honest searcher: ask with real intent, and the Spirit will manifest truth. (Moroni 10:4–5)

🌟

Line Upon Line

"Precept upon precept; here a little, and there a little." Understanding is layered, not downloaded. (2 Nephi 28:30)

📝

Write It Down

Nephi wrote for his soul's profit. Journaling your questions makes the answers legible when they finally arrive. (2 Nephi 4:15)

§ 02 · small things, great things

The Impact of Learning

"By small and simple things are great things brought to pass." (Alma 37:6) The human mind is a garden that grows in inches. Tap the spark for a daily nudge, then pick a habit that fits today.

You are capable of more than yesterday. Start here.

10 MIN

Read Something Unfamiliar

A scripture, a paragraph, a podcast. Strangeness is where the brain grows.

2 MIN

Write One Question

The question you carry all day is the one your mind will quietly answer.

5 MIN

Teach It Out Loud

If you can explain it to an empty room, you actually know it. If you can't, you've found your next lesson.

1 MIN

Pray Before You Study

A short, sincere prayer invites the Spirit to be your tutor — the best one you'll ever have.

15 MIN

Walk & Wonder

Da Vinci walked. Einstein walked. Nephi pondered. Motion unlocks what desks lock up.

3 MIN

Record Today's Insight

One sentence in a journal. Over a year that's 365 glimpses of a growing self.

7 MIN

Ask a Person Smarter Than You

Humility is the fastest shortcut in the world. Borrow a brain — they'll feel honored.

ALL DAY

Look for One Tender Mercy

Noticing trains the soul. Gratitude is the soil all learning grows in.
